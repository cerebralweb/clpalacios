Latin America Fintechs

Episode 3 of SmallData - Hexis' podcast - is dedicated to conversing with Carlos Gutierrez, investor and co-founder of INVX, a venture capital firm that has participated in startups like Tpaga and Rappi.

Carlos studied business administration at the Universidad de los Andes and has a Master in digital business from the University of Barcelona. Currently, he works as an entrepreneur at INVX; but before it was part of Wayra Colombia.

Late ... but interesting

The appointment was at 5pm, but I connected 25 minutes late. My interviewee, however, greets me with kindness and excuses me without further explanation; throughout the interview I understood that this flexibility was not accidental, but that my guest knew what it means to be patient (he is an investor) and he is also a fine analyst.

In the interview, Carlos tells how he got into the world of Venture Capital and why he was attracted by technology as an investment niche. We also spoke extensively about contemporary concepts such as Fintech, about angel investors and venture capital in technology startups.

The Airbnb Insights

Airbnb has a fortune in data. They come from the 200 million people who have booked trips on their platform and the 3 million users whose properties offer on the Internet. Despite this, the brand firmly believes that qualitative research is essential for the development of its products.

The Airbnb Insights Machine

Judd Antin - director of research for the brand - is in charge of the interesting task of converting shopping experiences into actionable insights for Airbab. Antin works with 57 experts who combine large sets of quantitative information with qualitative information. The strategic goal of insights is to "deeply understand our global guests and hosts well enough to make decisions", Antin said at the 2017 Strategy Festival of the American Association of Advertising Agencies. In his presentation, Antin talked about two projects that demonstrate how his Insights Department helps the company.

Judd Antin

Judd Antin is itself an interesting topic. On reading his linkedin, his scientific training is obvious: he completed a doctorate in information systems and studied psychology and anthropology; The latter is his basic training. At the bottom of its LinkedIn page there are several recommendations (this tool serves to display what other members of the platform would say about you).

Elizabeth Chuchill - Director of UX Research at Google - posted the following about Antin:

Judd and I have worked together in various capacities–he was my intern at Yahoo! Research, we collaborated informally, and on completion of his PhD, he joined my group, the Internet Experiences Group in Yahoo! Research. Judd is an amazingly talented researcher. He is deeply knowledgeable about his chosen areas of research and he's a gifted lateral thinker who can make connections and think creatively to solve problems. In addition to be an ideas person, he can execute to put theory into practice. He has the discipline to work rigorously toward grounded results and is willing to roll his sleeves up and get the everyday mundane work of moving from ideas to results. This make him the model applied scientist, able to have both short and long term impact in both academic and business contexts.

The Airbab Research Department is divided into three wings. Survey Science , obviously related to insight extraction from large data sets; User Experience (UX), which investigates the human computer relationship with digital ethnography techniques and Product Specialists who are dedicated to translating insights into specific strategies and solutions.
